How to Calculate a Tip

The standard formula is: Bill Amount x Tip Percentage = Tip Amount. Then add the tip to the bill for the total. For a $50 bill at 20%: $50 x 0.20 = $10 tip, making your total $60.

Standard Tipping Rates in the US

At restaurants, 15-20% is standard for table service. For exceptional service, 25% or more is generous. At bars, $1-2 per drink or 15-20% of the tab is customary. For delivery drivers, 15-20% with a minimum of $3-5. Hair salons typically receive 15-20% of the service cost. Hotel housekeeping usually receives $2-5 per night.

Quick Mental Math Tip

To calculate 20% quickly in your head: move the decimal point one place left (that's 10%), then double it. For a $45 bill: 10% = $4.50, doubled = $9.00 tip. For 15%, calculate 10% and add half again: $4.50 + $2.25 = $6.75.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is 20% tip on $50?
20% of $50 = $10.00 tip. Total bill with tip: $60.00.
Is 15% still an acceptable tip?
15% is considered the minimum for adequate service. 18-20% is now the standard. Below 15% signals poor service.
Should I tip on tax?
Technically, you should tip on the pre-tax subtotal. However, tipping on the total (including tax) is common and generous.
How much to tip for takeout?
10-15% for takeout is becoming standard, especially since 2020. For simple pickup, 10% is fine. For large or complex orders, 15-20%.
How to split a bill with different tips?
Each person calculates their own food/drink total, adds their preferred tip percentage, and pays that amount. This calculator handles equal splits.
