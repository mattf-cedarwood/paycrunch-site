What Does a Judge Do?

Judges oversee legal proceedings in courts of law, ruling on procedural matters, instructing juries, and issuing sentences.

How to Become a Judge

Education: Juris Doctor (J.D.) degree

Certifications: Bar admission; judicial appointment/election

Career path: Attorney → Magistrate/Judge → Senior Judge → Appellate Judge → Supreme Court Justice

AI & Judge: What You Need to Know in 2026

How AI Is Changing This Role

AI is modernizing judge work through intelligent document processing, data-driven policy analysis, and automated constituent services. While government adoption moves carefully due to transparency and equity requirements, AI tools are increasingly used to improve efficiency, reduce backlogs, and enhance public service delivery.

How Top Performers Use AI

  • Using AI-powered research and analysis tools to process large volumes of data, legal documents, and policy research more efficiently
  • Leveraging AI for constituent communication management, automated case routing, and predictive resource allocation
  • Employing data analytics platforms to identify trends, measure program effectiveness, and support evidence-based policy decisions

AI Skills That Help You Get Hired

Candidates who combine domain expertise with data literacy and AI tool proficiency stand out. Understanding responsible AI use, algorithmic bias considerations, and how to apply AI within regulatory and ethical frameworks is increasingly important for government roles.

AI Salary Impact

Judges with AI and data analytics skills earn 10-16% more, particularly in federal roles and agencies with active digital modernization programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a judge make?
▼
The national median salary for a judge is $148,030 per year, or $71.17 per hour. Entry-level positions start around $80,000 while top earners make $250,000 or more.
What education do you need to become a judge?
▼
Most judge positions require juris doctor (j.d.) degree. Additional certifications or experience may increase earning potential.
What is the job outlook for judges?
▼
Employment of judges is projected to grow 3% over the next decade, which is about average compared to the average for all occupations.
What are the highest paying states for judges?
▼
The highest paying states include Hawaii, California, New York, Massachusetts, and New Jersey, where cost of living adjustments push salaries above the national median.
Can you make six figures as a judge?
▼
Yes, experienced professionals in this field regularly earn six figures, especially in high-cost-of-living areas.
Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
