What Does a Professor Do?

Professors instruct at colleges and universities, develop curricula, conduct research, publish papers, and advise students.

How to Become a Professor

Education: Doctoral degree (PhD)

Certifications: No standard certs; publications essential

1. Earn a bachelor's degree.

2. Complete a master's degree.

3. Earn a PhD (4-7 years).

4. Gain teaching experience.

5. Publish and apply for tenure-track positions.

Career path: Adjunct → Assistant Prof → Associate Prof (tenured) → Full Prof → Department Chair → Dean

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a professor make?
▼

▼
Intellectual freedom and prestige but competitive job market.
How long does it take?
▼
10+ years: bachelor's, master's, PhD, and postdoctoral work.

Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Actual state-specific BLS data may differ. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
