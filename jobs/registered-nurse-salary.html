What Does a Registered Nurse Do?

Registered nurses provide and coordinate patient care, educate patients about health conditions, and provide emotional support. They work in hospitals, clinics, nursing homes, schools, and other healthcare facilities. Daily tasks include assessing patient conditions, administering medications, operating medical equipment, recording patient histories, and collaborating with physicians on treatment plans.

How to Become a Registered Nurse

Education: Bachelor's degree in Nursing (BSN)

Certifications: NCLEX-RN license required

1. Earn a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) from an accredited program (4 years). An Associate Degree in Nursing (ADN) is also accepted but BSN is increasingly preferred.

2. Pass the NCLEX-RN national licensing examination.

3. Obtain a state nursing license.

4. Gain clinical experience through residency programs or entry-level positions.

5. Consider specialty certifications (ICU, ER, pediatrics, oncology) for career advancement.

Career path: Registered Nurse → Charge Nurse → Nurse Manager → Nurse Practitioner or Director of Nursing

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a registered nurse make?
▼
The national median salary for registered nurses is $86,070 per year or $41.38 per hour. Salaries range from $61,250 (10th percentile) to $132,680 (90th percentile) depending on location, experience, and specialty.
What state pays registered nurses the most?
▼
California is the highest-paying state for registered nurses, with a median salary of approximately $124,000 per year. Other top-paying states include Hawaii, Oregon, Washington, and Massachusetts.
Is nursing a good career in 2026?
▼
Yes. The BLS projects 6% job growth for registered nurses through 2032, driven by an aging population and increased demand for healthcare services. The profession also offers strong job security and diverse specialty options.
How long does it take to become a registered nurse?
▼
It typically takes 2-4 years. An Associate Degree in Nursing (ADN) takes about 2 years, while a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) takes 4 years. Accelerated BSN programs for career changers take 12-18 months.
Can registered nurses make over $100,000?
▼
Yes. Many experienced nurses in high-paying states or high-demand specialties earn over $100,000. Travel nurses, nurse anesthetists, and nurses in management roles frequently exceed this threshold.
Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Actual state-specific BLS data may differ. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
