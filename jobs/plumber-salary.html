What Does a Plumber Do?

Plumbers install and repair piping systems for water, gas, drainage, and heating in buildings.

How to Become a Plumber

Education: High school diploma + apprenticeship (4-5 years)

Certifications: State plumber license

1. Earn a high school diploma.

2. Complete a plumbing apprenticeship (4-5 years).

3. Pass the journeyman exam.

4. Pursue master plumber certification.

5. Consider starting your own business.

Career path:Apprentice → Journeyman → Master Plumber → Contractor → Business Owner

AI & Plumber: What's Actually Changing in 2026

Smart buildings, tankless systems, water recycling, and IoT leak detection have transformed Plumber work from pure pipe-fitting into a hybrid of mechanical skill and technology integration. The Plumbers commanding premium rates in 2026 aren't just the ones who can sweat copper — they're the ones who can configure a smart water management system, diagnose issues remotely, and use AI-powered estimating to win jobs before the competition even sends a quote.

The Honest Risk Assessment

Plumbing is among the most AI-resistant professions — every building is different, fixtures fail in unpredictable ways, and the physical work requires judgment that no robot can match. The risk for Plumbers isn't automation, it's commoditization. As smart diagnostics make basic troubleshooting easier, the premium shifts toward complex installations (tankless, water treatment, smart building integration) and diagnostic expertise that simpler tools can't provide.

What This Means For Your Pay

Plumbers who can install and configure smart water systems, offer thermal leak detection, and provide AI-generated inspection reports earn $10-25/hr more than those limited to traditional service calls. The premium is even higher for Plumbers who service commercial buildings with building management system integration.

📋 Your Action Plan

A realistic, role-specific plan you can start this week:

Week 1: Digital estimating

Switch from handwritten or mental estimates to a digital estimating tool (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber). Generate professional on-site quotes for every job this week. Track your close rate vs. previous method.

Weeks 2-3: Diagnostic tech

If you don't have a thermal camera, rent one for a week and use it on every leak detection call. Calculate the ROI vs. exploratory cuts. Most plumbers who try thermal find it pays for itself in under a month.

Weeks 3-4: Smart water services

Get certified with one smart water monitoring brand (Phyn, Flo, or Streamlabs). Add it to your service menu. One installation per week at $500-800 is an extra $25-40K/year.

Month 2: Premium positioning

Update your Google Business Profile with photos of smart installations, thermal diagnostics, and professional inspection reports. The plumber with a professional online presence charges more than the one with a handwritten card.

Methodology and data sources
