What Does a Social Worker Do?

Social workers help people solve problems, assess needs, connect clients with resources, provide counseling, and advocate.

How to Become a Social Worker

Education: Bachelor's (BSW) or master's (MSW)

Certifications: State social work license (LSW, LCSW)

1. Earn a BSW (4 years).

2. For clinical practice, earn an MSW.

3. Complete supervised clinical hours.

4. Pass the state licensing exam.

5. Specialize.

Career path: Case Manager → Social Worker → Senior SW → Clinical Supervisor → Director

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a social worker make?
▼
The national median salary is $58,380 per year.
Is social work a good career?
▼
Meaningful work with 7% growth, though pay is modest for the education required.
How long does it take?
▼
4-6 years for BSW or MSW, plus 2-3 years for clinical licensure.
What is the difference between social worker and counselor?
▼
Social workers address environmental/systemic factors. Counselors focus on therapy.
What type of SW makes the most?
▼
Clinical social workers in private practice and healthcare.
Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Actual state-specific BLS data may differ. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
