What Does an Anesthesia Technician Do?

Anesthesia technicians assist anesthesiologists by preparing and maintaining anesthesia equipment, ensuring patient safety during surgical procedures.

AI & Anesthesia Technician: What You Need to Know in 2026

How AI Is Changing This Role

AI is transforming anesthesia technician roles through enhanced diagnostic accuracy, automated clinical documentation, and intelligent patient monitoring systems. While AI streamlines administrative burdens and augments clinical decision-making, the irreplaceable human elements of patient care, empathy, and complex clinical judgment keep this role essential.

How Top Performers Use AI

  • Using ambient AI scribes (like DAX Copilot or Abridge) to automate clinical documentation, saving 1-2 hours per shift
  • Leveraging AI-powered clinical decision support tools to cross-reference patient data against the latest research and flag potential issues
  • Employing predictive analytics platforms to identify high-risk patients early and optimize care plans

AI Skills That Help You Get Hired

Candidates who demonstrate proficiency in health informatics platforms, AI-assisted diagnostic tools, and electronic health record (EHR) optimization stand out. Understanding how to validate AI-generated clinical suggestions and integrate telehealth AI tools into patient workflows is increasingly valued by hiring managers.

AI Salary Impact

Anesthesia Technicians with health informatics and AI-assisted care skills typically command 12-18% higher salaries than peers without these competencies.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a anesthesia technician make?
▼
The national median salary for a anesthesia technician is $52,000 per year, or $25.00 per hour. Entry-level positions start around $35,000 while top earners make $74,000 or more.
What education do you need to become a anesthesia technician?
▼
Most anesthesia technician positions require associate's degree. Additional certifications or experience may increase earning potential.
What is the job outlook for anesthesia technicians?
▼
Employment of anesthesia technicians is projected to grow 10% over the next decade, which is faster than average compared to the average for all occupations.
What are the highest paying states for anesthesia technicians?
▼
The highest paying states include Hawaii, California, New York, Massachusetts, and New Jersey, where cost of living adjustments push salaries above the national median.
Can you make six figures as a anesthesia technician?
▼
While the median salary is below six figures, top earners in high-paying states and with significant experience can approach or exceed $100,000.
Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
