What Does a Dental Assistant Do?

Dental assistants work alongside dentists during procedures, prepare treatment rooms, sterilize instruments, take dental impressions and X-rays, and assist with patient education.

How to Become a Dental Assistant

Education: Postsecondary certificate or on-the-job training

Certifications: CDA certification preferred

1. Complete a dental assisting program (9-11 months).

2. Obtain state certifications.

3. Consider earning the CDA credential.

4. Get CPR/BLS certified.

5. Gain experience and specialize.

Career path: Dental Assistant → Expanded Functions DA → Dental Hygienist or Office Manager

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a dental assistant make?
▼
The national median salary is $44,820 per year or $21.55 per hour.
How long does it take to become a dental assistant?
▼
Programs take 9-11 months.
Is dental assisting a good career?
▼
Yes. Quick entry into healthcare with a path to dental hygienist.
What is the difference between a dental assistant and hygienist?
▼
Hygienists clean teeth, have more autonomy, and earn roughly double.
Do dental assistants take X-rays?
▼
Yes, in most states with proper certification.
Methodology and data sources

Salary data is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program. National median, 10th percentile, and 90th percentile figures are sourced from the most recent BLS OES release. State-level salary estimates are calculated by applying regional price parity adjustments from the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) to the national median. Actual state-specific BLS data may differ. Job growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. Education and certification requirements are based on BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook descriptions. All figures are approximate and updated periodically.
