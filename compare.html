Frequently Asked Questions

Where does the salary data come from?▼
All salary data is sourced from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics program. Entry-level salaries represent the 10th percentile, and senior-level salaries represent the 90th percentile of reported wages.
How are hourly rates calculated?▼
Hourly rates are calculated by dividing the annual salary by 2,080 hours (40 hours per week x 52 weeks per year), which is the standard full-time work schedule.
What does the AI Impact score mean?▼
The AI Impact score (1-10) estimates how much artificial intelligence and automation are expected to change a given occupation by 2030. A score of 1-3 means minimal impact, 4-6 means moderate changes, and 7-10 means significant transformation or disruption.
Can I share my comparison?▼
Yes! Click the "Copy Link" button to get a shareable URL. The link encodes both job selections so anyone who opens it will see the same comparison.
How many jobs can I compare?▼
This tool compares two jobs at a time. We have 1,006 occupations in our database, which means over 500,000 possible comparisons. Select any two from the dropdowns above.
