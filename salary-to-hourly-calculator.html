How to Convert Salary to Hourly Rate

To find your hourly rate from an annual salary, divide your salary by the total number of hours you work in a year. For a standard 40-hour week with no vacation: Annual Salary / 2,080 = Hourly Rate.

For example, a $50,000 salary / 2,080 hours = $24.04 per hour. With 2 weeks unpaid vacation, divide by 2,000 instead: $50,000 / 2,000 = $25.00 per hour.

When You Need This Calculation

This is useful when switching from a salaried role to freelancing or contract work, comparing a salary offer against an hourly position, or figuring out your true hourly value including overtime hours.

The Hidden Cost of Salary

Many salaried employees work more than 40 hours without extra pay. If you earn $60,000/year but work 50 hours/week, your effective hourly rate drops from $28.85 to $23.08. Always factor in actual hours worked when calculating your real rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is $50,000 a year hourly?
$50,000/year at 40 hours/week = $24.04/hour. Monthly: ~$4,167. Weekly: ~$962. Daily: ~$192.
What is $75,000 a year hourly?
$75,000/year at 40 hours/week = $36.06/hour. Monthly: ~$6,250. Weekly: ~$1,442.
What is $100,000 a year hourly?
$100,000/year at 40 hours/week = $48.08/hour. Monthly: ~$8,333. Weekly: ~$1,923.
Should I include benefits in salary?
For true comparison, yes. Health insurance ($5,000-$15,000/yr), retirement match, and PTO add 20-40% to your effective compensation.
What about overtime for salaried employees?
Salaried employees earning under $58,656/year (2024 threshold) are generally entitled to overtime pay. Above that threshold, you may be exempt.
